Unit 1: John 14:6

Understanding Latter-day Saint History and Doctrine: One of the most important things to keep in mind as we study the restoration of the gospel is that Jesus Christ is truth; that is, in our efforts to come to the truth, we must develop a relationsip with the Savior. We must recognize that we are not seeking after an absolute, abstract idealogy, but we are following a person - the only perfect person to have walked this earth.

Unit 2: Joseph Smith History 1:25

The First Vision: Joseph Smith knew what he knew, and he could not deny it. This particular excerpt comes from Joseph's 3rd primay account of the 4 total known. This account was written primarily as a part of the history of the church. Joseph's conviction regarding the experience he had as young boy, especially in the face of the adversity that came as a direct result of it, stands as a great testimony of the reality of the First Vision.

Unit 3: President Nelson on the Book of Mormon

The Book of Mormon: In the October 2017 General Conference, President Nelson gave his last conference address before becoming the Prophet. He relayed a powerful story in which an African tribal king, upon reading a passage in 3 Nephi, exclaimed, "You could have given me diamonds or rubies, but nothing is more precious to me than this additional knowledge about the Lord Jesus Christ." President Nelson then speaks to the power of the Book of Mormon, and encourages us to reflect on its importance to us.

Unit 5: Doctrine and Covenants 1:36-37

History and Purpose of the Doctrine and Covenants: Modern scripture is one of the greatest blessings from the restoration. In the first section of the Doctrine and Covenants, which is actually the __ section in chronilogical order, the Lord outlines one of the underlying themes of the restoration: "whether by mine own voice or by the voice of my servants, it is the same." The Lord's promise is that all of His words shall be fulfilled, including those given through His servants. We can have faith and trust in the words of the prophets as they are the Lord's mouthpiece on the earth today.
